# Business Plan: Summit Outdoor Gear Co-op

<!-- ANTIPATTERN: titled and structured as a business plan, but the
     Financial Plan below discloses no assumptions behind any projected
     figure — the mandatory disclosure this genre exists to require is
     simply missing. -->

## Executive Summary

Summit Outdoor Gear Co-op is going to be a huge success in the outdoor
retail space and we are asking for $400,000 to get started.

<!-- ANTIPATTERN: no company formation detail, no problem/solution framing,
     no traction — an unsupported success claim with no evidence anywhere
     in the document. -->

## Financial Plan & Projections

Year 1 revenue: $1,200,000. Year 2 revenue: $2,800,000. Year 3 revenue:
$5,000,000.

<!-- ANTIPATTERN: three years of revenue figures with no stated pricing,
     unit volume, growth rate, or cost basis behind any of them — a reader
     has no way to judge whether these numbers are grounded in anything or
     simply invented. There is also no income statement, cash flow
     statement, or balance sheet anywhere in the document — contrast with
     templates/good.md, which discloses all three in-document, not just
     referenced as "available on request." -->

## Funding Request

We need $400,000 as soon as possible.

<!-- ANTIPATTERN: a funding amount with no stated use of funds and no
     terms sought — the reader cannot evaluate what the money is for or on
     what terms it's being requested. -->

<!-- ANTIPATTERN, overall: no Market Analysis, no Competitive Analysis, no
     Operations Plan, no Management & Organization section, and no
     Appendix. The document also never states an SBA/SCORE
     convention-not-standard caveat, nor labels any figure as an
     assumption versus a sourced fact. -->
